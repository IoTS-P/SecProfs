As of January 2023, I've moved to Northeastern University in Boston where I am Professor of Electrical & Computer Engineering in the College of Engineering and the Khoury College of Computer Sciences. I am actively recruiting graduate students for advanced research in both medical device security (computing/bioengineering/public policy) and analog sensor cybersecurity (computer engineering, semiconductor design, physics).

My research vision is a world where science-based security is built-in by design to all embedded systems: medical devices, autonomous transportation, healthcare delivery, manufacturing, and the Internet of Things. This is challenging because the number of vulnerabilities continues to increase dramatically while solutions remain largely piecemeal and reactive. Finding systematic solutions that address root causes rather than symptoms of risk is extremely important now because consumers are surrendering control to IoT devices before the autonomous systems have adequate trustworthiness to withstand modern, constantly evolving adversaries and threat models.

I founded the Archimedes Center for Healthcare and Device Security. I direct the Security and Privacy Research (SPQR) group at Michigan. I previously served as the inaugural Acting Director of Medical Device Cybersecurity at FDA's Center for Devices and Radiological Health. I lead the Distinguished Speaker Series on Cybersecurity for Biomedical Engineering at the UCSF-Stanford Center for Regulatory Science and Innovation (FDA CERSI). I was a founding member of the N95decon.org team on decontamination science for emergency reuse of N95 filtering facepiece respirators during healthcare worker PPE shortages. I have an archived blog on medical device security and safety.
