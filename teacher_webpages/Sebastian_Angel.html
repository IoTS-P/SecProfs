I'm an assistant professor of Computer and Information Science at Penn where I'm part of the Distributed Systems Laboratory, the Security and Privacy Laboratory, and The Warren Center for Network and Data Sciences. I'm the recipient of an NSF CAREER Award, a JPMorgan Faculty Award, the ACM SIGOPS Dennis M. Ritchie Dissertation Award, and the Bert Kay Best Dissertation Award. I was previously a senior researcher at Microsoft Research.

I'm broadly interested in systems, security, privacy, and networking. My work aims to:

  • Build the next generation of privacy-preserving systems, from messasing apps to online advertising.
  • Bring accountability to online services by forcing these services to prove that they are doing the right thing.
  • Provide strong consistency and fault tolerance for large scale distributed systems at low cost.
